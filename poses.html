REFERENCES
Mountain Pose
  1. Stand straight with your weight evenly balanced throughout all parts of your feet.
  2. Follow your breath and as you are inhaling, take in a deep breath and lift your arms above your head and stretch upward with your palms facing each other.
Forward Bend
  1. Stand straight and slowly bend your body towards you knees while maintaining a flat back.
  2. Lower your hands to the floor and reach as far downward as you can.
Forearm Plank
  1. Lower yourself down onto your knees and hands.
  2. Place your forearms on the floor shoulder-width apart and position your knees hip-width apart.
  3. Push yourself away from the floor till you are standing on your forearms and your toes.
Downward Facing Dog (Down Dog)
  1. Lower yourself onto all fours and place your palms shoulder-width apart under your shoulders.
  2. Place your legs hip-width apart and closer towards your body than in a normal plank.
  3. Puch off the floor and position your face in between your hands so that you are looking at your thighs.
  4. Make sure to breathe.
Upward Facing Dog (Up Dog)
  1. Lower yourself onto the floor and place your palms shoulder-width apart and under your shoulders.
  2. Puch yourself off the floor with your hands and try to get your belly and chest off the floor with only the front of yout foot on the floor.
Warrior 1
  1. Stand straight and move your right or left leg back. Try to make the space between your two feet about the length of your leg.
  2. Move the leg that is behind into a 45 degree angle compared to your other leg and bend the leg in front of you at a 90 degree angle, but do not push youself if your knee or leg starts hurting.
Warrior 2
  1. Like in Warrior 1 move either your right or left leg back and make the space between both your feet at least the length of your leg.
  2. Move the leg behind you into a 180 degree angle and bend your leg in front of you into a 90 degree angle.
Warrior 3
  1. Put most of your wight onto one leg and begin lifting your other leg up till you are satisfied or you can make a 90 degree angle with both legs.
  2. Start moving both your hands in front of you till the insides of your elbows are at the same level as your head.
Chair Pose
  1. Stand straight and either place your feet hip-width apart or keep them together.
  2. Keep bending your knees while keeping your heels on the ground till you can go no further without seeing your toes.
  3. Move your arms till they are level with your head.
Child's Pose
  1. Bend your knees and sit on the backs of your feet.
  2. Reach your hands in front of your and place them on the floor while trying to completeley sit on you heels.
  3. Place your forehead on the floor and keep breathing.
Corpse Pose
  1. Lay on your back with your palms facing up.
  2. Keep your feet as wide apart as your mat and keep your arms away from your body.
  3. Close your eyes and relax all parts of your body.
